  • Year-End Donor Appeals & Impact Reports

    Creating compelling impact reports that highlight urban forestry achievements for annual fundraising campaigns

    Donor Communications Community Engagement

    Scenario

    You're assisting Groundwork Denver in writing a short impact section for their annual report highlighting their forestry program and achievements this past year. Provide some of the most impactful data for them to use in their report!

    Audience

    Development staff, Communications teams, Affiliate leadership

    What You'll Find

    • Total number of trees Denver planted in 2025 with support from the USDA-Forest Service grant
    • 2-3 environmental benefit metrics to highlight (e.g., CO2 removed, stormwater managed, air pollutants removed) with real-world comparisons (swimming pools of stormwater, Elephants' weight)
    • ROI ($) & Per-tree value ($) of trees planted this year

    What You'll Produce

    • Summary paragraph to describe program success
    • Design ideas for an infographic (or make one with a chart)
    • A call to action (e.g., your $X donation for trees goes to $X impact) for their report
    • Overview of the steps you took to produce your findings so they can follow the recipe next year

    Step-by-Step

    1. Filter by your affiliate using the Groundwork Affiliate
    2. Filter by planting year (e.g., 2025) using the Date Planted
    3. Filter to show only planted trees using the Tree Condition
    4. Filter by funding source (USDA grant) using the USDA Funded
    5. Review total tree count and key metrics in the Total Eco-Benefit Value widget
    6. Export environmental co-benefits data from the Individual Benefits
    7. Calculate cost-benefit ratio using the ROI widget
    8. Generate real-world comparisons (gallons → swimming pools, pounds → elephants)

  • Climate Resilience Grant Applications

    Supporting grant proposals with data on projected long-term climate impact of urban forestry investments

    Grant Writing

    Scenario

    You're applying for a $100K climate resilience grant focused on reducing urban heat and managing stormwater with new trees with Groundwork Rhode Island. The application requires you to include data about the projected long-term climate impact of their urban forestry work. Assume that your new project will plant 150 trees.

    Audience

    Development staff, Grant writers, Program managers

    What You'll Find

    • Total number of new trees planted across the Network
    • Total number of new trees that Groundwork Rhode Island has planted
    • Key climate benefits associated with stormwater and heat health impacts in Rhode Island
    • The average per-tree ($) value, & new tree ROI ($)
    • Estimate what the 25-year total impact will be for the 150 new trees using the average $ benefit per tree in Rhode Island (multiply!)

    What You'll Produce

    • Findings summarized in headline statements/bulleted list, emphasizing how these relate to climate resilience
    • Infographic plan (or creation) to help communicate the results visually
    • Step-by-step explanation of how to reproduce your findings for GWRI to use independently for future proposals

    Step-by-Step

    1. View Network-wide tree planting totals using the Total Eco-Benefit Value widget
    2. Filter to your affiliate using the Groundwork Affiliate
    3. Filter to show only planted trees using the Planted vs. Inventoried
    4. Review key climate benefits (stormwater, heat health) in the CO2 Removed from Atmosphere: Pounds widget
    5. Calculate average benefit per tree and ROI using the Runoff Avoided: Gallons per Tree widget
    6. Multiply average benefit × 150 planted trees to project future impact
    7. Export carbon sequestration data from the CO2 Equivalent of Carbon Stored: Pounds widget

  • Community Event Flyers & Neighborhood Storytelling

    Translating technical benefits data into accessible, memorable talking points for local audiences

    Community Engagement

    Scenario

    You're helping Groundwork Richmond, CA create a simple one-page flyer to hand out at an event to showcase their urban forestry efforts that will be attended by mostly volunteers and community members.

    Audience

    Communications teams, Event coordinators, Volunteer managers

    What You'll Find

    • How many new trees RCA has planted
    • Areas where RCA is planting a lot of trees (Street names, Parks, Schools) - zoom into the map to find places of interest!
    • Top climate benefits their trees provide - highlight three big ecobenefits and their real-world comparisons
    • Long-term impact of trees on a climate challenge (pick one: air pollution, urban heat health outcomes, or stormwater)
    • Average dollar value and ROI on new trees planted

    What You'll Produce

    • Outline the design of an infographic/one-page flyer with headline statements based on your findings
    • Identify social media/areas/neighborhoods/places of interest where these flyers could go
    • Consider how to make this more accessible to different audiences
    • Provide the steps and an explanation of how to recreate this for future events with other Affiliates in mind

    Step-by-Step

    1. Filter to your affiliate using the Groundwork Affiliate
    2. View total trees planted in the Total Eco-Benefit Value widget
    3. Zoom into the Map to identify high-concentration planting areas (streets, parks, schools)
    4. Review top 3 climate benefits in the Benefit Categories widget
    5. Select one climate challenge (air, heat, or flooding) and export long-term impact data
    6. Calculate average dollar value and ROI using the ROI
    7. Generate real-world comparisons for selected benefits

  • Tree Planting Strategy & Species Selection

    Using performance data to identify which species and locations deliver maximum environmental benefits

    Planning & Strategy

    Scenario

    You're assisting Groundwork Jacksonville with planning future urban forestry projects and helping them choose which tree species to use that maximize environmental benefits and community economic impact.

    Audience

    Program planners, Urban foresters, Affiliate leadership

    What You'll Find

    • Total number of trees that Jacksonville has recorded
    • Total number of new trees planted
    • Average $ value per new tree and their ROI
    • Top three beneficial tree species in Jacksonville as indicated by their Average 25-Year Benefit Value per species
    • Additional information about these Jacksonville-specific species from https://plants.usda.gov/ website (expected height, shade, hardiness, resistance to diseases)

    What You'll Produce

    • Draft infographic summarizing the average $ value per tree, ROI, and images of the top 3 local species
    • Key information about those species from the USDA website
    • Step-by-step guide on how to access this information in the dashboard for Jacksonville or other Affiliates

    Step-by-Step

    1. Filter to your affiliate using the Groundwork Affiliate
    2. View total tree count and planted trees in the Total Eco-Benefit Value
    3. Calculate average benefit per tree and ROI using the Benefit Value per Tree widget
    4. Review Network-wide top species using the Benefit Value per Species (Common Name) widget
    5. Filter to your affiliate and view local top species by average 25-year benefit value
    6. Export species list and research each on USDA Plants Database
    7. Compile species profiles with expected height, shade, hardiness, and disease resistance

  • City Budget Engagement & ROI Justification

    Providing municipal decision-makers with ROI data to justify continued investment in urban forestry programs

    City Partnerships

    Scenario

    You're assisting Groundwork Lawrence in making the case for their City to increase budgetary investment in trees for their community. The City Manager is more concerned about potholes than tree health!

    Audience

    Advocacy staff, City partnership coordinators, Affiliate leadership

    What You'll Find

    • Number of trees recorded
    • Number of new trees planted
    • Key environmental co-benefits relevant to city infrastructure and public health
    • Total ecobenefits value ($), per-tree value ($), and ROI for new trees ($)
    • Groundwork Lawrence's per-tree 25-year cost of maintenance

    What You'll Produce

    • Slides about Groundwork Lawrence's forestry impact, highlighting the budgetary need and longevity of tree program
    • Explanation that ROI includes projections of future maintenance, and without that support, the 25-year impacts may reduce or disappear
    • Proposed annual budget amount to support the maintenance of GWL's new trees
    • Step-by-step instructions on how to access this information for Groundwork Lawrence to use in the future

    Step-by-Step

    1. Filter to your affiliate using the Groundwork Affiliate
    2. View total trees and planted trees in the Groundwork Affiliate widget
    3. Review infrastructure-relevant benefits (stormwater management, pavement protection) in the Runoff Avoided: Value widget
    4. Calculate per-tree ROI including future maintenance projections
    5. Estimate annual maintenance costs using regional USDA community forestry guide data
    6. Export public health benefits (air quality, heat illness reduction)
    7. Create presentation showing 25-year benefit trajectory with/without maintenance

  • Media Interviews & Public Health Messaging

    Arming spokespeople with credible, cite-able statistics for press interviews and public statements

    Media & Press

    Scenario

    A national news reporter has reached out for an interview during a heat wave that is affecting nearly the entire country. Provide some sticky public health-specific information about the Groundwork Network's new trees and 25-year impact potential.

    Audience

    Communications teams, Spokespeople, Affiliate leadership

    What You'll Find

    • Number of new trees planted
    • Health-relevant metrics for these new trees' 25-year impact: the number of avoided illnesses and deaths
    • Additional benefits related to air quality improvement in a real-world comparison
    • Total Value per tree and ROI on new trees planted

    What You'll Produce

    • Three talking points and one memorable 'catch phrase' for the affiliate to use in their interview
    • Suggested infographic design to share the impact data using a chart or picture
    • Step-by-step explanation of how to reproduce your findings

    Step-by-Step

    1. View Network-wide planted trees in the Groundwork Affiliate
    2. Review heat health metrics (hospitalizations avoided, deaths prevented) in the Heat Illness Avoided: Cases widget
    3. Export 25-year cumulative health impact projections
    4. Review air quality improvement data in the Air Pollutants Removed: Pounds widget
    5. Calculate total ROI for new tree investments using the ROI widget
    6. Craft talking points emphasizing health impacts and economic value
    7. Develop memorable comparison (e.g., "preventing X hospital visits annually")

  • Estimating Future Project Impact for Proposals

    Using existing tree performance data to project the climate impact of proposed new planting projects

    Grant Writing Planning & Strategy

    Scenario

    Proposed tree planting projects need projected impact estimates to justify investment. Use per-tree averages from your existing inventory to forecast benefits from planned plantings.

    Audience

    Grant writers, Program planners, Development staff

    What You'll Find

    • Per-tree average metrics for your affiliate (gallons per tree, carbon per tree, pollutants per tree)
    • Benefit Value per Tree ($) for calculating total economic value
    • Maintenance per Tree costs for ROI calculations
    • Species-specific performance data if you have planned species mix

    What You'll Produce

    • Project proposal showing estimated benefits over 25 years
    • Projected ecosystem services (stormwater, carbon, air quality, heat)
    • Estimated economic value and ROI for proposed planting
    • Appropriately caveated projections based on your existing forest performance

    Step-by-Step

    1. Filter to your affiliate using the Groundwork Affiliate
    2. Filter to new plantings only using the Planted vs. Inventoried
    3. Calculate average 25-year benefit per tree in your region
    4. Multiply average benefit × proposed tree quantity (e.g., 150 trees)
    5. Break down projected benefits by category (carbon, stormwater, air quality, heat)
    6. Calculate projected ROI using regional per-tree maintenance costs
    7. Export projections for inclusion in grant narrative
